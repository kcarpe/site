Bio

Kay Carpender recently retired as the Assistant Director of both the USA Center for Rural Public Health Preparedness and the Office of Special Programs & Global Health at the Texas A&M University School of Public Health. With over 35 years of experience in the field of public health, Carpender has managed a wide range of initiatives from retrospective and prospective epidemiologic studies to leading a team of public health professionals to develop and implement activities related to public health preparedness. Carpender uses a wide range of techniques, including Rural Preparedness Roundtables, didactic learning (face-to-face and online), and public health emergency exercises to assist communities in building preparedness and response capacity.

For the past 12 years, Carpender has served as a consultant with the South Dakota Department of Health to address public health preparedness challenges in tribal communities within the Sioux Nation in South Dakota. During this time, she has developed strong working relationships with partners at the South Dakota Department of Health, as well as the leadership within several Sioux Tribes. Based on this work, Carpender recently began to work with the 3 tribes in Texas to facilitate meetings to build relationships between the state health department and each of those tribes.

Carpender is now offering her expertise through a consulting business specializing in tribal public health preparedness. Technical assistance offered includes community facilitation, as well as public health preparedness planning and exercise.
